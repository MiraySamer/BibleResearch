In 1857 a milestone was reached when Robert Moffat completed a translation of the Bible into Tswana, a language of southern Africa.
He also printed it in sections on a handpress. This was the first complete Bible to be printed in Africa and was also the first complete translation into a formerly unwritten African language.

 1st Bible

The earliest translations of the whole Bible into an African language were done in Egypt. Known as the Coptic versions, they are believed to date from the 3rd or 4th century.
About three centuries later, the Bible was translated into Ethiopic.

 History

First Bible entered Australia with the arrival of European settlers in the late 18th century, during the First Fleet that landed in Botany Bay in 1788. The First Fleet was the 1st group of ships that transported convicts, marines & officers from Great Britain to the newly established British colony in Australia.

First Fleet Arrival (1788): which carried about 1,500 people, also brought with it Christian religious materials, including the Bible. The first chaplain of the colony, Richard Johnson, was a key figure in introducing Christianity to the new colony.

Richard Johnson was the 1st Anglican chaplain appointed to the colony. He carried a Bible with him and played a central role in the religious life of the early settlement. His mission was not only to provide spiritual guidance but also to lead religious services, including Sunday worship.
